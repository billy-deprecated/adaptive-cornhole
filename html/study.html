User Study

Study Design

We ran a study with four different users testing our game. They all did a training session with both static and adaptive settings and a testing session immediately following each training session; two of them started with the static setting, and the other two started with adaptive.

Training
  1. Static condition:
    1. 15 shots on easiest setting
    2. 15 shots on the hardest setting
  2. Adaptive condition:
    1. 30 shots, while board auto-adjusts
Testing
  1. (Both conditions) Participant takes 15 shots on hardest setting.
Quantitative Results

Half of our users showed better performance immediately following their adaptive training session. One user performed worse, and the other showed no signifcant difference.

Score was calculated using cornhole rules: 1 point per board land, 3 points for going through the hole.

  1. Better after adaptive training

User 2: Adaptive first

  2. Score worse by 10 point

Qualitative Results

Overall, users expressed they had fun playing and recognized its merit. All considered the idea to be novel and interesting, despite however they might have performed.

One takeaway from our conversations with them was that more transparency would be appreciated-- in other words, being very clear about when and how the board was expected to move. Another idea we got from the users was giving the player more control over the adjustments made, i.e the ability to control what moves, like choosing to hold the hole size constant and plaing with the distance adjusting faster than usual.

Qualitative Results

Our hypothesis was that training with the adaptive setting would help produce better performance in the testing session that followed compared to the static setting.
